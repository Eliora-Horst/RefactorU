Jokes from The Book of Bulls

Baited with two Centuries of bold facts, or nimble lies, OR

A combat between Sence and Non-sence, being at strife who shall infuse most Myrth into the gentle-Reader - 1636

One passing the Ferry at Hampton Court, the Ferry mans wife at the time row'd the Boat; whereat he wondering, said to his companion, Lord bless us, I never in my life saw a woman ferry-man before.
A sick man askt another how he did : I am well sayes hee as you, or any body else can bee that is no better in health then I am.
Of disoursing of the succession of Popes, what brave worthy men some of them had beene, and what villains others had prov'd themselves: but for the Pope then living, how good, and wise man he was; a subtle stander by put this questions, I pray you my Masters (quoth hee, ) if this Pope die withouth a sonne, who shall be Pope next?
A Lord walking in a Church were all his ancestors were buries, and old servant of his then present said, O my Lord, what worthy men they were, I sweare if I live I will be buried close by them.
One seeing a Peare-tree very full, wondered what the owner could doe with all those Peares ; why said another to him he sels them to the Bakers to make penny apples pies of them.
A Skullion having washt his hands very white; I marry quoth he this is somewhat like, but if I ahd washed my hands in Snow water, then they would have beene whiter.
